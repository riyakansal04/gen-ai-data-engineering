Context:
I am a data engineer working on a Snowflake data warehouse.
We have a fact table fact_sales with 1.2 billion rows joined with 3
dimension tables. This query is taking 45+ minutes during nightly ETL:

    SELECT c.customer_name, p.product_category, d.fiscal_quarter,
           SUM(f.revenue) AS total_revenue, COUNT(f.sale_id) AS total_orders
    FROM fact_sales f
    JOIN dim_customer c ON f.customer_id = c.customer_id
    JOIN dim_product p ON f.product_id = p.product_id
    JOIN dim_date d ON f.sale_date = d.date_key
    WHERE f.sale_date BETWEEN '2023-01-01' AND '2023-12-31'
    GROUP BY c.customer_name, p.product_category, d.fiscal_quarter
    ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;

Role:
Act as a senior Snowflake data warehouse optimization expert
with 10+ years of experience tuning large-scale analytical queries.

Task:
Analyze and rewrite the above SQL query to maximize performance.
Identify bottlenecks and explain what changes you made and why.

Constraints:

- Do not change the output columns or business logic
- Focus on performance improvements only
- Assume clustering keys and warehouse size can be adjusted

Format:
